Reddit NLP Project

Using reddit comments and posts talking about the shows Seinfeld and Curb you Enthusiasm I was able to use Natural Language Processing techniques to categorize the posts. I first used reddit's API to pull in the posts and comments from the two shows and by using the API I was able to pull in thousands of posts and comments from each show. I then cleaned my data by getting rid of all instances of websites and non-alphabetical characters as these characters didn't help in predicting which show the post was about. To predict the show I used a number of methods to break apart the words and determine the show. I first used count vectorizer and logistic regression as the base model and was able to get an 86 percent accuracy score on my testing data, which is significantly higher than my 50 percent baseline score. I was able to increase my accuracy by using the TFIDF Vectorizer and Multinomial Naive Bayes and Random Forest models and was able to get accuracy scores of 90 and 88 percent. Measuring the Economic Impact of Natural Disasters

I was tasked with finding if there is any sort of negative relationship with instances of natural disasters and how successful a cities economy does. For this project my team and I decided to study the affect Hurricane Sandy had on local wages and unemployment statistics in 10 different cities along the east coast shore. We collected a number of different weather statistics to measure how severly the storm hit the city and found monthly unemployment statistics for wage and unemployment for each city. I wanted to use a time series model to model the data because I had historical data. Because natural disasters occur suddenly, I was trying to assess the sudden fluctuations in the economic data that may have occurred in the months following Hurricane Sandy, which occurred in October 2012. That means that, in the context of ARIMA modeling, I was interested in extracting the moving average component of this data (which handles sudden fluctuations), and ignoring the auto-regressive component of this data (which handles long-term trends). Because I had obvious seasonality in this data, I took one order of seasonal differencing (12 months) before fitting the models. In general, I did not see any obvious fluctuation in the moving average wages for any locality and for any industry.As opposed to wages, I saw a small uptick in unemployment at the beginning of 2013 that may potentially be attributed to Hurricane Sandy, but it is not a large enough increase for me to make any meaningful conclusions.
